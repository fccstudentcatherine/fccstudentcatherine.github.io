Web Site vs Web App

(Week 2) Site vs App The end user may not be able to tell the difference between a website and a web app. Not all places that we go to by typing in a URL are websites. Web sites are purely informational like blogs or news outlets. I prefer msn.com (Links to an external site.) but you can choose from most news websites and get the idea that they are where we go to read information. What these sites have in common is that they have very little interaction with the end user. They simply display information and links to more information for people to view. Web apps on the other hand actually interact with the user. They may also provide information but they often customize information for the user. An example of this would be a banking app. I use Bank of America or bofa.com (Links to an external site.). While I can visit their site to find out general information I can also log in to gain access to my personal information that no one else has access to. Build it and they will come I’m not currently in the market to create a web page or web app but eventually I would like to do something with my photography hobby/side-job. I would like to be able to post galleries for people to view, share, order and download. I currently share photos using Google Drive but I run into space restrictions and it is just file sharing and not something that I can control the content that surrounds the photos. It would be nice to have an aesthetically pleasing and functional web app. I’d like for people to be able to log into their own personal galleries as well as have access to general information and samples of my work. There could be categories of theme or age groups and other information like a normal website but then have the log in and custom features of a web app.
